About

Safi Ahmed Memon

Assistant Professor of Mechanical Engineering · NUST, Islamabad

I have a background in computational modeling of physical systems — from designing cooling systems for AI chips to solving fluid flow problems in industrial water filtration. My work sits at the intersection of classical mechanics and modern computational tools.

I attended the Wolfram Summer School (2024) where I worked directly with Stephen Wolfram and Wolfram's lead PDE developers to model coupled flow systems. I was previously a BK21 Postdoctoral Fellow at Kyungpook National University in South Korea, and I now teach at NUST, Pakistan's top engineering university.

My approach is practical: I help engineers and researchers build models that are reusable, teachable, and fast to iterate on — using Wolfram Mathematica as the primary tool.
